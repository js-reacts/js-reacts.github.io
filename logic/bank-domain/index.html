    Bank Domain

    In a bank like Nepal Bank Limited or any other commercial bank in Nepal, the Treasury Department is a crucial unit responsible for managing the bank’s financial assets, liabilities, and overall liquidity.

    Overview

    Here's a breakdown of what it is and what it does:

    What is the Treasury Department in a Bank?

    The Treasury Department is the part of the bank responsible for:

    • Managing liquidity (ensuring the bank has enough cash to meet its obligations)
    • Investing excess funds
    • Managing foreign exchange (forex) transactions
    • Controlling interest rate and market risks
    • Maintaining regulatory compliance related to cash reserves and capital

    Functions of the Treasury Department in Nepal Bank

    Here are the main functions in more detail:

    1. Liquidity Management 💰 तरलता व्यवस्थापन

    2. Ensures the bank has enough money to meet daily withdrawals and payments.

    3. Manages short-term funding and borrowing from other banks or the central bank.

    4. Foreign Exchange (Forex) Operations 🌍 विदेशी मुद्रा कारोबार

    5. Deals with buying and selling of foreign currencies.

    6. Manages exchange rate risk.

    7. Supports customers in foreign trade transactions (import/export).

    8. Investment Management 📈 लगानी व्यवस्थापन

    9. Invests the bank’s surplus funds in government securities (like bonds or treasury bills).

    10. Looks for low-risk opportunities to earn income while preserving capital.

    11. Interbank Transactions 🏦 इन्टरबैंक कारोबार

    12. Deals with borrowing/lending money to/from other banks to manage short-term cash needs (interbank market).

    13. Asset-Liability Management (ALM) ⚖️ सम्पत्ति–दायित्व व्यवस्थापन

    14. Balances the bank's assets (like loans) and liabilities (like deposits).

    15. Ensures that interest rate risk and maturity mismatches are minimized.

    16. Compliance and Reporting 🧾 नियमन र प्रतिवेदन

    17. Complies with Nepal Rastra Bank (NRB) regulations.

    18. Maintains required reserve ratios (CRR, SLR).
    19. Submits reports to regulators.

    Summary

    Module Direct Treasury Functions Tied
    Forex Exchange Rate Forex Operations, Compliance, ALM
    Money Market Deal Liquidity, Interbank Transactions, Investment, ALM
    Mutual Fund Transfer Investment, ALM, Compliance
    Non-Deal Fund Liquidity, Compliance, ALM

    📚 Core Domain Knowledge Areas for Nepal Bank Treasury Department

    1. Monetary Policy & Central Banking (Nepal Rastra Bank - NRB)

    • NRB monetary policy instruments: repo, reverse repo, SLF, OMOs
    • Statutory Liquidity Ratio (SLR), Cash Reserve Ratio (CRR)
    • NRB directives and circulars
    • Interactions with NRB for liquidity support and reserve requirements

    2. Liquidity & Funds Management

    • Managing daily liquidity to meet obligations
    • Forecasting inflows/outflows of funds
    • Interbank lending and borrowing
    • Cash flow planning and position management

    3. Foreign Exchange Management

    • Understanding of foreign currency transactions and settlements
    • Managing FCY reserves (USD, EUR, INR, etc.)
    • Dealing with forex positions and trading (spot, forward)
    • Regulatory compliance in forex per NRB guidelines

    4. Investment & Portfolio Management

    • Investments in government securities (T-bills, bonds)
    • Repo and reverse repo operations
    • Assessing yield, risk, and duration of securities
    • NRB auctions and primary dealership (if applicable)

    5. Risk Management

    • Interest rate risk, liquidity risk, and forex risk management
    • Value-at-risk (VaR), gap analysis
    • Asset-Liability Management (ALM)
    • Stress testing and scenario analysis

    6. Banking Systems and Technology

    • Use of core banking systems (e.g., Finacle, Flexcube)
    • SWIFT transactions for cross-border payments
    • Real-Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) system of NRB
    • Electronic bidding in NRB's OMOs

    7. Regulatory & Compliance Knowledge

    • NRB unified directives on treasury operations
    • Basel III and capital adequacy implications
    • KYC/AML compliance in treasury transactions
    • Audit requirements (internal and NRB)

    8. Financial Markets in Nepal

    • Structure of Nepali money and capital markets
    • Participants: NRB, commercial banks, development banks, NRB-licensed BFIs
    • Yield curve knowledge and interest rate trends
    • Interbank call money market

    9. Accounting & Reporting

    • Mark-to-market (MTM) valuation of securities
    • Treasury P&L management and reporting
    • IFRS/NFRS application in treasury instruments
    • Daily MIS and reports to management/NRB
    🛠️ Tools Commonly Used
    • Bloomberg (if subscribed)
    • NRB reporting tools (e.g., online portal for OMOs)
    • Excel for modeling & analysis
    • Bank’s treasury module in core banking

    🔄 Currency Conversion Rule of Thumb

    Knowing when to multiply or divide during currency conversion is essential to avoid mistakes. Here's a simple rule to follow:

    ✅ Use this basic formula:

    Home currency = Foreign currency × Exchange rate (if rate is given as “1 unit of foreign = X units of home”)

    OR

    Foreign currency = Home currency ÷ Exchange rate

    💡 Ask yourself:

    “Am I converting to or from my home currency?”

    Let’s say you're in Nepal (home currency = NPR) and you have:

    Example 1: You want to buy USD using NPR

    Exchange rate: 1 USD = NPR 133

    You're converting NPR → USD, so:

    ✅ Divide:

    If you have NPR 13,300 → USD = 13,300 ÷ 133 = USD 100

    Example 2: You have USD and want to get NPR

    • Exchange rate: 1 USD = NPR 133

    ✅ Multiply:

    If you have USD 100 → NPR = 100 × 133 = NPR 13,300

    🔁 General Rules

    Converting Action Example
    Foreign → Home ✅ Multiply USD × 133 = NPR
    Home → Foreign ✅ Divide NPR ÷ 133 = USD

    📌 Important Notes:

    • Always check which direction the rate is quoted in (e.g., 1 USD = 133 NPR or 1 NPR = 0.0075 USD)

    • Banks may show both buying and selling rates (you buy foreign at a higher price, sell at a lower one)

    • For example, a bank may quote:

    • USD Buying: 132.50 (bank buys from you)

    • USD Selling: 133.50 (bank sells to you)

    Additional

    🔁 Do the Multiply/Divide Rules Apply the Same for Buying and Selling?

    Yes — the math rule (multiply/divide) stays the same, but you must use the correct rate:

    • Buying: You're buying foreign currency → use the higher (selling) rate
    • Selling: You're selling foreign currency → use the lower (buying) rate

    📌 Let’s break it down:

    Scenario Action Use This Rate Math Example (USD/NPR 132.5/133.5)
    You want to buy USD Divide NPR Selling rate NPR ÷ 133.5 13,350 ÷ 133.5 = 100 USD
    You want to sell USD Multiply USD Buying rate USD × 132.5 100 × 132.5 = 13,250 NPR

    ✅ Summary: When converting currency:

    1. The math (multiply/divide) doesn't change
    2. What does change is the rate you apply (buying vs selling)
    3. Divide when converting from home to foreign (you’re giving more NPR to get 1 USD)
    4. Multiply when converting from foreign to home (you’re receiving NPR for each USD)
    # Multi-currency conversion tool with static example rates

# Example exchange rates (you can update these daily)
exchange_rates = {
    "USD": {"buying": 132.50, "selling": 133.50},
    "EUR": {"buying": 142.00, "selling": 143.50},
    "INR": {"buying": 1.60, "selling": 1.65},
}

def convert_currency(amount, from_currency, to_currency, rates):
    if from_currency == "NPR":
        # Buying foreign currency (you give NPR)
        rate = rates[to_currency]["selling"]
        converted = amount / rate
        transaction = f"Buying {to_currency}"
    elif to_currency == "NPR":
        # Selling foreign currency (you get NPR)
        rate = rates[from_currency]["buying"]
        converted = amount * rate
        transaction = f"Selling {from_currency}"
    else:
        return "Conversion must involve NPR as either source or target currency."

    return {
        "transaction_type": transaction,
        "original_amount": amount,
        "converted_amount": round(converted, 2),
        "rate_used": rate
    }

# 🧪 Examples

print(convert_currency(13350, "NPR", "USD", exchange_rates))  # Buying USD
print(convert_currency(100, "USD", "NPR", exchange_rates))    # Selling USD

print(convert_currency(14350, "NPR", "EUR", exchange_rates))  # Buying EUR
print(convert_currency(100, "EUR", "NPR", exchange_rates))    # Selling EUR

print(convert_currency(1650, "NPR", "INR", exchange_rates))   # Buying INR
print(convert_currency(1000, "INR", "NPR", exchange_rates))   # Selling INR

    Deals

    1. Forex Exchange Rate

    🎯 Functionality:

    • View and update daily exchange rates for currencies (USD, EUR, INR, etc.)
    • Base currency (e.g., NPR) vs foreign currencies

    • Effective date, buying/selling rate

    • Money Market Deal

    🎯 Functionality:

    • Record interbank lending/borrowing
    • Deal type (Call/Notice/Money Market), Counterparty, Interest rate

    • Maturity date, Amount, Deal date

    • Mutual Fund Transfer

    🎯 Functionality:

    • Transfer of units between banks or internal portfolios

    • Fund name, units, value per unit, source, destination

    • Non-Deal Fund

    🎯 Functionality:

    • Cash inflow/outflow not tied to any deal
    • Examples: interest received, settlement funds, internal adjustments
